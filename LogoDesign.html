"Not Your Wa1fu" logo is a design inspired by the Twitch streamer NotYourWa1fu and her branding. I designed three different logos for NotYourWa1fu to choose from, then created brand elements and markers to compliment them. I used Adobe Illustrator to design these logos below.

The floral brand elements used are designed by pch.vector/Freepik.

Three Logo Variations

Thought Process

As per the brand identity, I wanted to incorporate script font for a soft, cute design. To give more variety from the first logo, I decided on a minimalist sans-serif font for the second logo that was video game themed to represent NotYourWa1ifu's Twitch content. For the third logo I combined both script and sans-serif for a unique, versatile logo to choose from.

The client decided on the completed logo that contains the sans-serif and script font due to its more professional design. It came across as a logo that could be diverse in whatever direction her brand could take. She did not request any further changes to the logo.

"The Apprent1ce" logo is a design inspired by the Twitch streamer The_Apprent1ce and her branding. I designed three different logos for The_Apprent1ce to choose from, then created brand elements to compliment them. I used Adobe Illustrator to design these logos below.

Thought Process

As per the brand identity, I wanted to use serif fonts that were sophisticated with a dark tone to them. I used two different serif fonts between the three logo designs. When deciding on the designs, I knew I would want one specific to chess as a majority of her content revolves around chess. The other two logos are more centered around her personality than on her current content so she had an option of variety to her personal brand.

The client decided on the completed logo that contains the chess piece design. She also enjoyed the plant iconography in one of the other logos, so I incorporated that into her Twitch banner design. She did not request any further changes to the logo.

"Adair Conlin" is a personal logo design I created with a minimalist design in mind. For my personal logo, I wanted it to be minimal and symmetrical while showcasing both "art" and "design" relative to my brand. I used Adobe Illustrator to design this logo.
